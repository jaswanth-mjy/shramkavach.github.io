3. The Board of Peace: A New Paradigm for Middle East Reconstruction

Following the stabilization of Venezuela, the axis of global diplomacy shifted to the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland. On January 22, 2026, a historic ceremony marked the ratification of the Board of Peace, an initiative designed to bypass traditional diplomatic stalemates through direct economic intervention.

3.1 The Davos Accord: Charter and Structure

A "CEO-style" Approach to Statecraft

The Board of Peace is not a United Nations body; it is an independent international organization established to oversee the reconstruction, demilitarization, and governance of Gaza. Its structure reflects a hybrid public-private partnership model, prioritizing efficiency, accountability, and resource mobilization.

The Board acts as a transitional authority, with President Donald Trump serving as its Chairman. The Executive Board comprises a mix of seasoned diplomats, intelligence chiefs, and global business leaders.

Infrastructure: Deep-water port and new international airport to connect Gaza directly to the world economy.

Tourism: Luxury tourism sector generating employment and foreign exchange.
