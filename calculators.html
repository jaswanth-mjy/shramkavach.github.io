GST (Goods & Services Tax) Explained

GST is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It is a destination-based tax - meaning tax is applied where consumption happens.

üî¢ GST Rates in India:

  • 0%: Essential items (milk, bread, books)
  • 5%: Necessities (tea, edible oil, transport)
  • 12%: Standard goods (computers, processed food)
  • 18%: Most services (IT, consulting, hotels)
  • 28%: Luxury items (cars, AC, tobacco)

üìä GST Components:

  • CGST: Central GST (to Central Government)
  • SGST: State GST (to State Government)
  • IGST: Integrated GST (inter-state transactions)
  • Example: 18% GST = 9% CGST + 9% SGST

üí° When to Register: Freelancers and gig workers must register for GST if annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ20 lakh. For services: ‚Çπ20 lakh (‚Çπ10 lakh for special category states).

üìù Two Types of Calculation:

GST Exclusive: When you have base price (‚Çπ1,000) and need to add 18% GST ‚Üí Final: ‚Çπ1,180

GST Inclusive: When final price includes GST (‚Çπ1,180) and you need to extract base ‚Üí Base: ‚Çπ1,000

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment that you make every month to repay your loan. It includes both principal amount and interest.

üî¢ EMI Formula:

EMI = [P √ó R √ó (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]

Where: P = Principal, R = Monthly Interest Rate, N = Number of months

üí° Key Points:

  • ‚úì Initially more interest is paid
  • ‚úì Later more principal is paid
  • ‚úì Higher EMI = Less total interest
  • ‚úì Longer tenure = Lower EMI but more interest

üìä Typical Interest Rates:

  • Home Loan: 8.5%-9.5% p.a.
  • Car Loan: 9%-11% p.a.
  • Personal Loan: 10.5%-24% p.a.
  • Education Loan: 8%-12% p.a.

üí∞ Pro Tip: Your EMI should not exceed 40-50% of your monthly income for healthy finances. Exceeding this can cause financial stress.
