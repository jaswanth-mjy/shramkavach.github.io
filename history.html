Major periods that shaped India's history

Ancient India (3300 BCE - 600 BCE)

Indus Valley Civilization & Vedic Period

Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BCE): One of the world's oldest urban civilizations with advanced city planning in Harappa and Mohenjo-daro. Featured sophisticated drainage systems, standardized weights, and trade networks.

Vedic Period (1500-600 BCE): Composition of the Vedas, foundation of Hindu philosophy, development of Sanskrit literature. Introduction of caste system and establishment of early kingdoms.

Urban Planning Sanskrit Vedic Literature
üëë

Classical Age (600 BCE - 1200 CE)

Mauryan & Gupta Empires

Mauryan Empire (322-185 BCE): Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, reached its peak under Emperor Ashoka. First empire to unify most of the Indian subcontinent. Ashoka's conversion to Buddhism and spread of Dhamma.

Gupta Empire (320-550 CE): Golden Age of India - advances in mathematics (zero, decimal system), astronomy, medicine, literature, and arts. Aryabhata, Kalidasa flourished during this period.

Buddhism Spread Golden Age Scientific Progress
üïå

Medieval India (1200 - 1707)

Delhi Sultanate & Mughal Empire

Delhi Sultanate (1206-1526): Islamic rule established, Indo-Islamic architecture developed. Qutub Minar, Alauddin Khilji's military campaigns, administrative reforms.

Mughal Empire (1526-1857): Founded by Babur, reached zenith under Akbar, Shah Jahan, and Aurangzeb. Taj Mahal, Red Fort, synthesis of Indian and Persian cultures. Rich artistic and architectural heritage.

Taj Mahal Cultural Synthesis Architecture

British Colonial Era (1757 - 1947)

Company Rule & British Raj

East India Company (1757-1858): Battle of Plassey 1757, gradual expansion of British control, exploitation of resources, introduction of railways and telegraph.

British Raj (1858-1947): Direct British rule after 1857 Revolt, economic drain, social reforms, rise of nationalism, Indian National Congress formation (1885).

Railways Nationalism Freedom Movement
üóΩ

Freedom Struggle (1857 - 1947)

Path to Independence

Major Movements: 1857 Revolt (First War of Independence), Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), Civil Disobedience Movement (1930), Quit India Movement (1942).

Key Leaders: Mahatma Gandhi (Non-violence), Jawaharlal Nehru, Sardar Patel, Subhas Chandra Bose, Bhagat Singh, and countless freedom fighters who sacrificed their lives.

Independence Day: August 15, 1947 - India gained freedom, becoming the world's largest democracy.

Non-Violence Civil Disobedience Democracy
üöÄ

Modern India (1947 - Present)

Independent Democratic Republic

Constitution (1950): World's longest written constitution, established India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic.

Economic & Social Progress: Green Revolution, White Revolution, IT boom, space program (Chandrayaan, Mangalyaan), nuclear capability, economic liberalization (1991).

21st Century: Digital India, startup ecosystem, global IT hub, world's fastest-growing major economy, Mars mission success.

Constitution IT Revolution Space Missions
