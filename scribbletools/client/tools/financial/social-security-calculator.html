2024 Social Security Key Facts

Important Numbers for 2024:
• Maximum Taxable Earnings: $160,200
• Maximum Monthly Benefit at Full Retirement Age: $3,822
• Average Monthly Benefit: $1,767
• Cost-of-Living Adjustment (COLA): 3.2% increase from 2023
• Earnings Test Limit (under FRA): $22,320 annually
Benefits are adjusted annually for inflation using the Consumer Price Index!

Full Retirement Age by Birth Year

  • Born 1937 or earlier: Full retirement age is 65
  • Born 1938-1942: Full retirement age increases gradually to 66
  • Born 1943-1954: Full retirement age is 66
  • Born 1955-1959: Full retirement age increases gradually to 67
  • Born 1960 or later: Full retirement age is 67

Impact of Claiming Age on Benefits

Benefit Adjustments by Claiming Age:
• Age 62: 75% of full benefit (25% reduction)
• Age 65: ~93% of full benefit (varies by birth year)
• Full Retirement Age: 100% of full benefit
• Age 68: 108% of full benefit (8% increase)
• Age 70: 124% of full benefit (24% increase)
Delayed retirement credits stop accruing at age 70!

How Social Security Benefits Are Calculated

  • 35 Highest Years: Benefits based on your 35 highest-earning years
  • Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME): Adjusted for wage inflation
  • Primary Insurance Amount (PIA): Calculated using benefit formula
  • Bend Points: Progressive formula favors lower earners
  • Annual Adjustments: COLA increases protect against inflation
  • Maximum Benefits: Capped based on maximum taxable wages

Strategies to Maximize Benefits

  • Work at Least 35 Years: Avoid zeros in your benefit calculation
  • Maximize Earnings: Higher lifetime earnings = higher benefits
  • Delay Claiming: Earn 8% per year from FRA to age 70
  • Check Your Record: Review annual statements for accuracy
  • Consider Spousal Benefits: May be higher than your own benefit
  • Plan Around Taxes: Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable

Spousal and Survivor Benefits

Benefits for Spouses and Survivors:
• Spousal Benefit: Up to 50% of worker's full benefit
• Survivor Benefit: Up to 100% of deceased spouse's benefit
• Divorced Spouse: May claim on ex-spouse's record (married 10+ years)
• Children's Benefits: Up to 50% for dependent children under 18
• Family Maximum: Limits total benefits paid on one record
Claiming strategies can significantly impact total lifetime benefits!

Social Security Taxes and Withholdings

  • FICA Tax Rate: 6.2% employee + 6.2% employer = 12.4% total
  • Self-Employment: Pay both portions (12.4%) on Schedule SE
  • Taxable Wage Base: $160,200 maximum in 2024
  • Medicare Tax: Additional 1.45% each (no wage cap)
  • High Earners: Extra 0.9% Medicare tax on income over $200K
  • Credits Earned: Need 40 quarters (10 years) for retirement benefits

When Social Security May Be Taxed

Federal Income Tax on Benefits:
• Single, income $25,000-$34,000: Up to 50% of benefits taxable
• Single, income over $34,000: Up to 85% of benefits taxable
• Married filing jointly, $32,000-$44,000: Up to 50% taxable
• Married filing jointly, over $44,000: Up to 85% taxable
• 13 states also tax Social Security benefits
Income includes wages, pensions, investment income, and half of SS benefits!

Common Social Security Mistakes

  • Claiming Too Early: Permanent reduction in monthly benefits
  • Not Checking Records: Errors can reduce your benefit calculation
  • Ignoring Spousal Benefits: May be higher than own work record
  • Forgetting About Taxes: Benefits may be subject to income tax
  • Working After Claiming: Earnings test may reduce benefits before FRA
  • Not Coordinating with Spouse: Joint claiming strategies can maximize benefits

Future of Social Security

  • Trust Fund Status: Reserves projected to be depleted by 2034
  • Benefit Reductions: Without action, benefits could be cut ~20%
  • Proposed Solutions: Raise cap, increase taxes, adjust benefits
  • Planning Assumption: Most experts expect modifications, not elimination
  • Younger Workers: Should plan for potential changes in retirement age/benefits
  • Political Reality: Social Security remains popular and politically protected
