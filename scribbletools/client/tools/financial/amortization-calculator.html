Amortization Calculator

About the Amortization Calculator

The Amortization Calculator helps you understand your loan repayment schedule by breaking down each payment into principal and interest components. This tool is essential for planning major purchases like homes, cars, or education loans.

What is Amortization?
Amortization is the process of paying off a loan with regular payments over time. Each payment includes both principal (the original loan amount) and interest (the cost of borrowing). Over time, the portion of each payment that goes toward principal increases while the interest portion decreases.

How to Use This Calculator:
  1. Loan Amount: Enter the total amount you're borrowing (e.g., $250,000 for a mortgage)
  2. Annual Interest Rate: Enter the annual interest rate as a percentage (e.g., 4.5%)
  3. Loan Term: Enter the loan term in years (e.g., 30 years for a typical mortgage)
  4. Click Calculate: View your monthly payment and complete amortization schedule
  5. Download Excel Schedule: Get the full payment schedule in Excel format for your records

What You'll Discover:
  • Monthly Payment: Your fixed monthly payment amount
  • Total Payment: Total amount paid over the life of the loan
  • Total Interest: Total interest paid over the loan term
  • Payment Breakdown: How much of each payment goes to principal vs. interest
  • Remaining Balance: How your loan balance decreases over time

Perfect For:
  • Home Buyers: Understanding mortgage payments and comparing loan options
  • Auto Loans: Calculating car payments and total interest costs
  • Student Loans: Planning repayment strategies and understanding interest costs
  • Personal Loans: Evaluating loan offers and repayment schedules
  • Refinancing: Comparing current loan with refinancing options

Pro Tips for Loan Planning:
  • Compare loan terms: See how shorter terms save interest but increase monthly payments
  • Consider extra payments: Even small additional payments can significantly reduce total interest
  • Check interest rates: Small rate differences can mean thousands over the loan term
  • Understand fixed vs. variable: Fixed rates provide stability, variable rates may start lower but can increase
  • Factor in insurance and taxes: For mortgages, remember these additional costs

Common Loan Scenarios:
  • 30-year Mortgage: Lower monthly payments but higher total interest
  • 15-year Mortgage: Higher monthly payments but significant interest savings
  • Auto Loan (5 years): Typical term for new vehicle financing
  • Student Loan (10 years): Standard repayment plan for federal student loans

Note: This calculator provides estimates for planning purposes. Actual loan terms may vary based on credit history, fees, and other factors. Consult with financial advisors for personalized loan advice.
