Retirement Savings Fundamentals

The Power of Starting Early:
• Compound Interest: Your money earns returns, which then earn returns
• Time Advantage: Starting 10 years earlier can double retirement wealth
• Dollar-Cost Averaging: Regular contributions smooth out market volatility
• Habit Formation: Early saving builds lifelong financial discipline
Example: $200/month from age 25-65 vs $400/month from age 35-65 - same total contributions, but early starter has significantly more wealth!

Common Retirement Savings Guidelines

  • 10x Rule: Save 10-12 times final salary by retirement
  • Age-Based Milestones: Save 1x salary by 30, 3x by 40, 6x by 50, 8x by 60
  • 4% Rule: Plan to withdraw 4% of retirement savings annually
  • 80% Replacement: Need 80% of pre-retirement income
  • 15% Savings Rate: Save 15% of gross income including employer match
  • 50/30/20 Rule: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings (including retirement)

Investment Return Expectations

Historical Market Returns (1926-2023):
• Stocks: ~10% average annual return (high volatility)
• Bonds: ~5-6% average annual return (lower volatility)
• Mixed Portfolio: 7-8% for balanced 60/40 stocks/bonds
• Conservative Estimate: Use 6-7% for planning purposes
• Inflation Impact: Real returns ~3-4% after 3% inflation
Past performance doesn't guarantee future results - use conservative estimates!

Types of Retirement Accounts

  • 401(k): Employer-sponsored, often with matching (up to $23,000/year in 2024)
  • Traditional IRA: Tax-deductible contributions, taxed in retirement ($7,000/year limit)
  • Roth IRA: After-tax contributions, tax-free growth and withdrawals
  • SEP-IRA: Self-employed retirement accounts (up to 25% of income)
  • 403(b): Non-profit and government employee retirement plans
  • Taxable Accounts: No contribution limits but no tax advantages

Maximizing Employer Benefits

  • Company Match: Always contribute enough to get full employer match
  • Vesting Schedules: Understand when employer contributions become yours
  • Auto-Escalation: Increase contributions automatically each year
  • Rollover Strategy: Move 401(k) when changing jobs to maintain growth
  • Loan Provisions: Understand 401(k) loan rules and risks
  • Investment Options: Choose low-fee funds within your plan

Age-Based Investment Strategies

Asset Allocation by Life Stage:

20s-30s (Growth Phase):
• 80-90% stocks, 10-20% bonds
• Focus on growth and long-term wealth building

40s-50s (Accumulation Phase):
• 70-80% stocks, 20-30% bonds
• Balance growth with some stability

60s+ (Preservation Phase):
• 50-70% stocks, 30-50% bonds
• Shift toward income and capital preservation
Rule of thumb: Stock percentage = 100 - your age

Catch-Up Contributions (Age 50+)

2024 Contribution Limits (with catch-up):
• 401(k): $23,000 + $7,500 catch-up = $30,500 total
• Traditional/Roth IRA: $7,000 + $1,000 catch-up = $8,000 total
• SEP-IRA: Up to $69,000 (25% of compensation)
• Simple IRA: $16,000 + $3,500 catch-up = $19,500 total
Use catch-up contributions if you're behind on retirement savings!
