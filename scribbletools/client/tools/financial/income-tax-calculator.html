2024 Federal Tax Brackets

Tax Rates for Single Filers:
• 10%: $0 - $11,000
• 12%: $11,001 - $44,725
• 22%: $44,726 - $95,375
• 24%: $95,376 - $182,050
• 32%: $182,051 - $231,250
• 35%: $231,251 - $578,125
• 37%: $578,126+
Progressive tax system means you pay different rates on different income portions.

Understanding Tax Terms

  • Gross Income: Total income before any deductions or taxes
  • Adjusted Gross Income (AGI): Gross income minus above-the-line deductions
  • Taxable Income: AGI minus standard or itemized deductions
  • Marginal Tax Rate: Tax rate on your last dollar of income
  • Effective Tax Rate: Average tax rate on all your income
  • Tax Liability: Total amount of tax you owe

Filing Status Comparison

  • Single: Unmarried or legally separated individuals
  • Married Filing Jointly: Married couples combining incomes (often most beneficial)
  • Married Filing Separately: Spouses file separate returns (rare advantage)
  • Head of Household: Unmarried with qualifying dependents (better rates than single)
  • Qualifying Widow(er): Surviving spouse with dependent child (2 years after death)

Tax Planning Strategies

  • Maximize 401(k) Contributions: Reduce taxable income with pre-tax retirement savings
  • Traditional IRA Deductions: Up to $7,000 deductible ($8,000 if 50+)
  • HSA Contributions: Triple tax advantage with health savings accounts
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Offset capital gains with investment losses
  • Timing Income: Defer bonuses or accelerate deductions when beneficial
  • Itemize vs Standard: Compare to maximize deductions

Common Deductions & Credits

2024 Standard Deduction Amounts:
• Single: $14,600
• Married Filing Jointly: $29,200
• Head of Household: $21,900

Popular Tax Credits:
• Child Tax Credit: Up to $2,000 per child
• Earned Income Tax Credit: Up to $7,430 (varies by income/family size)
• American Opportunity Tax Credit: Up to $2,500 for education
Credits reduce tax owed dollar-for-dollar, more valuable than deductions!

Quarterly Estimated Payments

  • Required if you'll owe $1,000+ in taxes after withholding
  • Due dates: January 15, April 15, June 15, September 15
  • Safe harbor: Pay 100% of last year's tax (110% if AGI > $150K)
  • Avoid underpayment penalties with timely estimated payments
  • Self-employed individuals typically need quarterly payments

State Income Tax Considerations

State Tax Landscape:
• No State Income Tax: AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY
• Highest State Rates: CA (13.3%), NY (10.9%), NJ (10.75%)
• Some states tax only dividends/interest income
• Consider total tax burden when relocating
State taxes can significantly impact your overall tax liability!
