Loan Calculator

About the Loan Calculator

The Loan Calculator helps you estimate your monthly payments and understand the total cost of borrowing. This versatile tool works for personal loans, student loans, business loans, and other types of installment loans.

How Loans Work:
Loans are borrowed money that you repay with interest over a set period. Each payment includes both principal (the original loan amount) and interest (the cost of borrowing). Over time, more of your payment goes toward principal as the loan balance decreases.

How to Use This Calculator:
  1. Select Loan Type: Choose Personal, Student, or Business loan for tailored calculations
  2. Loan Amount: Enter the total amount you're borrowing
  3. Interest Rate: Enter the annual interest rate as a percentage
  4. Loan Term: Enter the loan term in years
  5. Advanced Options (Optional): Include origination fees and extra payments
  6. Click Calculate: View your monthly payment and complete payment schedule
  7. Download Excel Schedule: Get the full payment schedule for your records

What You'll Discover:
  • Monthly Payment: Your fixed monthly payment amount
  • Total Payment: Total amount paid over the life of the loan
  • Total Interest: Total interest paid over the loan term
  • Payment Breakdown: How much of each payment goes to principal vs. interest
  • Early Payoff Impact: How extra payments reduce your loan term and interest

Perfect For:
  • Personal Loans: Planning for debt consolidation, home improvements, or major purchases
  • Student Loans: Understanding repayment options and total education costs
  • Business Loans: Budgeting for equipment, inventory, or expansion financing
  • Debt Planning: Comparing loan offers and choosing the best financing option
  • Early Payoff Strategies: Seeing how extra payments can save money

Pro Tips for Borrowing:
  • Compare APRs, not just rates: APR includes fees and gives the true cost of borrowing
  • Consider shorter terms: They mean higher payments but less total interest
  • Check for prepayment penalties: Ensure you can pay off the loan early without fees
  • Understand all fees: Origination fees, late fees, and other charges add to the cost
  • Make extra payments when possible: Even small amounts can significantly reduce interest

Common Loan Scenarios:
  • Debt Consolidation: Combining high-interest debts into one lower-rate loan
  • Home Improvement: Financing renovations that increase your home's value
  • Student Loan Repayment: Planning payments after graduation
  • Business Equipment: Financing necessary tools or technology
  • Major Purchase: Spreading the cost of a large expense over time

Note: This calculator provides estimates for planning purposes. Actual loan terms may vary based on credit history, lender requirements, fees, and other factors. Always consult with financial advisors for personalized loan advice.
