2024 Estate Tax Exemptions

Federal Estate Tax:
• Exemption Amount: $13.61 million per person ($27.22 million for married couples)
• Tax Rate: 40% on amounts above exemption
• Only 0.2% of estates pay federal estate tax

Key Fact: The federal exemption is scheduled to drop to ~$6 million in 2026
Most families won't owe federal estate taxes, but state taxes may still apply!

States with Estate or Inheritance Taxes

  • Estate Tax States: Connecticut, Hawaii, Illinois, Maine, Massachusetts, Minnesota, New York, Oregon, Rhode Island, Vermont, Washington, DC
  • Inheritance Tax States: Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Nebraska, New Jersey, Pennsylvania
  • Both Estate & Inheritance: Maryland and New Jersey have both taxes
  • Lower Exemptions: State exemptions range from $1 million to $12.92 million
  • No State Taxes: 38 states have no estate or inheritance taxes

What's Included in Your Estate

  • Real Estate: Primary residence, vacation homes, rental properties
  • Financial Accounts: Bank accounts, investment portfolios, retirement accounts
  • Business Interests: Ownership stakes in companies or partnerships
  • Personal Property: Vehicles, jewelry, art, collectibles
  • Life Insurance: Death benefits from policies you own
  • Debts: Outstanding mortgages and loans reduce estate value

Estate Planning Strategies

Common Tax Reduction Techniques:
• Annual Gift Tax Exclusion: $18,000 per recipient (2024)
• Lifetime Gift Exemption: $13.61 million (shares federal exemption)
• Charitable Deductions: Unlimited deduction for qualified charities
• Marital Deduction: Unlimited transfers to surviving spouse
• Generation-Skipping Trusts: Transfer wealth to grandchildren tax-efficiently
Strategic gifting during lifetime can significantly reduce estate taxes!

Advanced Estate Planning Tools

  • Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs): Remove life insurance from taxable estate
  • Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): Transfer appreciation to beneficiaries
  • Qualified Personal Residence Trusts (QPRTs): Discount value of residence transfers
  • Family Limited Partnerships: Transfer business interests at discounted values
  • Charitable Remainder Trusts: Income stream plus charitable deduction
  • Dynasty Trusts: Multi-generational wealth transfer vehicles

Valuation Considerations

  • Fair Market Value: Price willing buyer would pay willing seller
  • Discounts Available: Minority interests, marketability restrictions
  • Professional Appraisals: Required for certain assets like businesses, art
  • Date of Death Values: Assets valued as of date of death
  • Alternate Valuation: Executor can choose 6 months after death
  • Special Use Valuation: Farms and businesses may qualify for discounts

Estate Tax vs Inheritance Tax

Key Differences:

Estate Tax:
• Paid by the estate before distribution
• Based on total estate value
• Paid before beneficiaries receive assets

Inheritance Tax:
• Paid by individual beneficiaries
• Rates vary by relationship to deceased
• Spouses often exempt, children get lower rates
Some states have both taxes, requiring careful planning!

When to Consult Professionals

  • Estate Value > $5 Million: Complex planning strategies may be beneficial
  • Business Ownership: Succession planning requires specialized expertise
  • Multiple State Connections: Different state laws create complications
  • Blended Families: Complex beneficiary structures need careful planning
  • Charitable Goals: Tax-efficient charitable giving strategies
  • Tax Law Changes: 2026 exemption reduction requires action

Essential Estate Planning Documents

  • Will: Directs asset distribution and names executor
  • Revocable Living Trust: Avoids probate, provides privacy
  • Financial Power of Attorney: Manages finances if incapacitated
  • Healthcare Power of Attorney: Makes medical decisions
  • Healthcare Directive: Documents end-of-life wishes
  • Beneficiary Designations: Keep retirement accounts and insurance current

Common Estate Planning Mistakes

Avoid These Costly Errors:
• Not updating beneficiary designations after major life events
• Procrastinating on estate planning until it's too late
• Failing to coordinate state and federal tax strategies
• Not considering generation-skipping tax implications
• Overlooking annual gifting opportunities
• Inadequate liquidity planning for tax payments
Proper planning can save your heirs significant taxes and complications!
