About Standard Deviation

Standard Deviation (σ or s): Measures the spread of a set of numbers from their mean.
Mean (μ or x̄): The average of the numbers.
Variance (σ² or s²): The average of the squared differences from the mean.

Formulas:
  • Mean: μ = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) / n
  • Variance (Population): σ² = Σ(xᵢ - μ)² / n
  • Variance (Sample): s² = Σ(xᵢ - x̄)² / (n-1)
  • Standard Deviation: σ = √σ² or s = √s²
Use Cases:
  • Understanding data spread and consistency
  • Comparing variability between datasets
  • Statistical analysis in science, business, and research
