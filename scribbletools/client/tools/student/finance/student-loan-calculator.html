Smart Borrowing Tips

Compare Federal vs Private Loans

Federal loans often have lower interest rates, flexible repayment options, and forgiveness programs. Always max out federal aid before considering private loans.

Only Borrow What You Need

Every dollar borrowed must be repaid with interest. Create a realistic budget and borrow only for essential education expenses.

Make Interest-Only Payments

During school or grace periods, pay interest if possible. This prevents interest capitalization and reduces your total repayment amount.

Pay Extra When Possible

Even small extra payments can significantly reduce total interest and shorten your loan term. Apply extra payments to principal when possible.

Understand Loan Forgiveness

Research loan forgiveness programs for public service, teaching, or other qualifying careers. These can save thousands in loan repayment.

Consider Refinancing

After graduation with good credit, refinancing can lower your interest rate. However, be aware you may lose federal loan benefits.
