How to Use the GPA Calculator

Step 1: Choose your GPA type (Semester or Cumulative).
Step 2: Select your GPA scale (4.0 for standard, 5.0 for weighted/honors courses).
Step 3: If calculating cumulative GPA, enter your previous GPA and credits.
Step 4: Click "Add Course" to add each of your current courses.
Step 5: Enter the course name, select the letter grade, and enter credit hours.
Step 6: Click "Calculate GPA" to see your results.

Understanding GPA Types

Semester GPA

Calculates your GPA for one specific term (semester or quarter). This shows how well you performed during that particular academic period.

Cumulative GPA

Calculates your overall GPA across all semesters combined. Enter your previous GPA and total credits to see your updated cumulative GPA.

Understanding GPA Scales

4.0 Scale (Unweighted)

Standard scale used by most high schools and colleges. All classes have equal weight.

A = 4.0
A- = 3.7
B+ = 3.3
B = 3.0
B- = 2.7
C+ = 2.3
C = 2.0
C- = 1.7
D+ = 1.3
D = 1.0
F = 0.0

5.0 Scale (Weighted)

Weighted scale for Advanced Placement (AP), honors, or IB courses. Gives extra points for challenging classes.

A = 5.0
A- = 4.7
B+ = 4.3
B = 4.0
B- = 3.7
C+ = 3.3
C = 3.0
C- = 2.7
D+ = 2.3
D = 2.0
F = 0.0

GPA Calculation Formula

Semester GPA Formula:

GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Hours

Cumulative GPA Formula:

Cumulative GPA = (Previous Quality Points + Current Quality Points) ÷ (Previous Credits + Current Credits)

Pro Tips:
• Quality Points = Grade Points × Credit Hours for each course
• Most colleges require a minimum 2.0 GPA to remain in good standing
• A 3.5+ GPA is considered excellent and may qualify for honors
• Track your GPA each semester to monitor your academic progress
• Remember: weighted GPAs can exceed 4.0 on the 5.0 scale!
