Frequently Asked Questions

An advance payment request is a formal letter asking your employer or client to provide a portion of your salary or project payment before the scheduled payment date. It's typically used for urgent financial needs or project-related expenses.
The amount depends on your company's policy. Generally, salary advances range from 30% to 80% of your monthly salary. For project advances, it's typically 20-50% of the project value. Always check your company's advance payment policy.
Most companies don't charge interest on salary advances as they're deducted from your next salary. However, some organizations may charge a nominal processing fee. Project advances are usually interest-free as they're part of the agreed payment terms.
Processing time varies by organization. Emergency advances may be processed within 2-3 days, while regular advances take 5-7 working days. Project advances depend on approval hierarchy and may take 7-15 days.
For medical emergencies: hospital bills, prescriptions. For education: fee receipts. For home repairs: quotations from contractors. For project work: purchase orders, expense estimates. Documents strengthen your request and speed up approval.
Yes, requests can be rejected if: you have pending advances, the amount exceeds company limits, insufficient reason, or company financial constraints. If rejected, you can reapply with modifications or wait for the next cycle.
Repayment options include: single deduction from next salary, monthly installments (2-12 months), deduction from project payment, or from bonus/incentive. Choose based on your financial capacity and clearly mention it in your request.
Most organizations don't allow multiple simultaneous advances. You must clear your existing advance before requesting a new one. However, in exceptional circumstances (like medical emergencies), management may consider special approval.
Salary advances are not separately taxable as they're part of your regular salary. Tax deduction (TDS) happens when your full salary is processed. For project advances, the full payment amount is taxed as per income tax rules when the project is completed.
The outstanding advance amount will be deducted from your final settlement, which includes pending salary, leave encashment, and gratuity. If the settlement amount is insufficient, you may need to repay the balance directly before receiving your relieving letter.
Yes! Freelancers can request project advances before starting work or at milestones. Typical advance ranges are 20-50% for new clients, and up to 70% for established relationships. Always have a written agreement specifying advance terms, deliverables, and repayment conditions.
